Assignment 2: Data Exploration

For this visualization I used a data set from Kaggle based on the World Happiness Report. This report explores happiness around the world based on different "social foundations for individuals and nations" (worldhappiness). The overall Happiness Score data is displayed, along with Economy, and Trust. Economy is based on GDP per capita and Trust is the overall trust of the citizens and their government.

I decided to visualize the data with a circle packing graphic. The circle packing layout allowed the data to be grouped based on regions of the world. The different regions were encoded with a color that showed a group of circles were part of the same group. Space was also utilized to show what the groups were. A legend helps identify what color pertains to the region of the world. Within these regions the different country values, encoded in the size of the circles, could be compared with each other. The larger the circle, the higher this social factor contributed to the overall Happiness Score. As one hovers over the circles, the name of the specific country displays.

The Happiness Score controller, encodes the overall Happiness Score for specified countries. The larger the size of the circle, the higher the country scored. Based on the region groupings, you can identify which regions overall have a high Happiness Score and within those regions, which countries have a higher overall Happiness Score. The Economy controller, helps identify which regions and countries have high or low Economy scores. The Trust controller, like the other controllers, helps identify which regions and countries have high or low Trust scores.

When comparing the three visuals when changing the controllers, it is interesting how the overall Happiness Score visual has relatively same sized circles. When the Economy and Trust controllers are toggled, there are move obvious differences. For example, the Sub-Saharan Africa region in the Economy visual is easily identifiable as the region with the most variation in GDP per capita. Another example, in the Trust visual, within the different regions, there are several countries that have high trust and then countries on the opposite end.
